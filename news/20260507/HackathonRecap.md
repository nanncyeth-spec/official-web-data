## [Announcement] HashKey Chain Horizon Hackathon: A Full Retrospective

Mar 10 – Apr 23, 2026
<br></br>
Forty-four days. Four tracks. Four hundred and fifty builders. And twelve teams who will help shape what gets built on HashKey Chain next.
<br></br>
The Horizon Hackathon is over. Here is a full account of what happened.
<br></br>
By the Numbers
Before anything else, the data:
- 450+ registered developers from across the globe
- 220+ projects submitted
- 160+ projects advanced to Round 1 evaluation
- 28 teams selected for Demo Day
- 12 winning teams across four tracks
- 4 AMAs with 40,000+ reach and exposure
- 60+ ecosystem partners
- 200K+ views on X

This was not a small experiment. It became one of the largest coordinated builder events HashKey Chain has run.
<br></br>
The AMA Series: Building in Public, Four Times Over
<br></br>
The hackathon did not start with a form. It started with a conversation.
We ran four X Space AMAs across the six weeks of the hackathon, each one timed to a different phase of the builder journey, and each one tackling a different question builders actually had.
<br></br>
- AMA #1, Mar 19: Hackathon Kickoff
<br></br>
Everything you needed to know before you started building. HashKey Chain Core Builder Alex walked through the four tracks in detail. AWS's David Sung covered what cloud infrastructure was available to teams. DoraHacks' James Ward explained exactly how submissions and judging would work. 153,000 tuned in.
<br></br>
- AMA #2, Mar 26: The Future of Autonomous Payments
<br></br>
With PayFi as one of the four tracks, we brought in builders who are already shipping in the space. HashKey On-Chain BG's Vector on the R&D side; Huy Vo from TinyFish on ML-driven payment infrastructure; Shawn from AllScale on what real-world autonomous payment deployment looks like. 145,000 tuned in.
<br></br>
- AMA #3, Apr 2: AI Meets On-Chain
<br></br>
The longest AMA of the series at 1:25. The question on the table was whether AI x crypto is the real narrative of the next cycle or another wave of hype. Eric from HashKey DEX, Jason Shen from Articuler.ai, and Adam from GoPlus Security each brought a different angle. The conversation ran long because the answers were not simple. 116,000 tuned in.
<br></br>
- AMA #4, Apr 15: What It Takes to Win
<br></br>
Held on submission deadline day. The focus shifted from what to build to what happens after. Alex from HashKey Chain, Luna from ETHPanda, and Kaelyn from Plutos Capital covered what judges look for, how post-hackathon support works, and what the path from hackathon winner to funded project actually looks like. 97,900 tuned in.

<br></br>
Developer and Technical Support
<br></br>
Getting 450+ developers to build production-quality applications on a new chain in six weeks requires more than a prize pool.
<br></br>
HashKey Chain provided builders with direct access to chain documentation, technical onboarding resources, and infrastructure from AWS. Teams could build, test, and deploy on HashKey Chain with cloud support from Day 1.
<br></br>
The pre-screening process, run through a combination of AI-assisted evaluation and human review, gave every submission a structured read across six dimensions: track fit, project completeness, team quality, solution value, authenticity, and code quality. Of 220+ submissions, 160+ cleared the first round. It was not a filter designed to cut projects. It was a signal to teams about where their submissions stood before Demo Day.
<br></br>
28 teams made it through.
<br></br>
Demo Day and the Judging Panel
<br></br>
April 22. AWS HK Office, Tower 535, 17/F, Causeway Bay.
<br></br>
28 teams. Seven-minute slots. One full day, 10AM to 5PM.
<br></br>
Teams presented live, in person in Hong Kong or streaming online. Every submission was evaluated on its merits: what it built, whether it worked, whether it solved a real problem, and whether it was actually deployable on HashKey Chain.
<br></br>
The judging panel brought together expertise from across the ecosystem:
- Huidong ZHANG,  Senior R&D Engineer, HashKey Chain
- Amio,  Director, HashKey DEX
- Alex,  Co-Founder, DGrid AI
- Neo,  Senior Web3 Solutions Architect, Amazon Web Services
- Kenny,  Senior Solutions Architect, Amazon Web Services
- Vaelyn,  Portfolio Manager / Research, Plutos Capital

Six judges. Four tracks. Independent scoring, averaged per team.
